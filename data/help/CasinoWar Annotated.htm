Casino War Game Rules

 

Brief Description

Casino War is a variation of the War game you might have played as a child. The player and the dealer each get one card, and the high card wins. If the cards are identical, you can choose to battle it out by going "to war".

 

Number of Players

This game is usually played on a standard blackjack table with one dealer and between one and seven players.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

This game is usually played with six standard 52-card decks, no jokers, and no wildcards.

 

Objective

To have the highest card and therefore win the "war".

 

How Suits Are Ranked

Suit rankings are not used in this game.

 

How Casino War is Played

 

1.      Each player places the opening bet before the first card is dealt.

2.      The cards are ranked as Ace being highest, 2 lowest.

3.      The dealer deals one card to each player and to himself, all face-up.

4.      One of the following will then happen:

·        If the dealer's card is higher than a player's card, that player loses his bet.

·        If a player's card is higher than the dealer's card, that player wins even money.

·        If the dealer's card matches a player's card, the player must either

·        Surrender (thereby losing half of his original bet, OR

·        Go to War (see instructions below).

 

Going To War

1.      To wage war, the player must double his original bet.

2.      The dealer will burn (put out of play, face down) three cards.

3.      The dealer will then deal one card to the player and one card to himself.

4.      One of the following will then happen:

·        If the dealer's second card is higher than the player's second card, that player loses both his original and additional bets.

·        If a player's second card is higher than (or matches) the dealer's second card, that player wins only his original bet.

 

Other Considerations and Options

•     Cards are dealt clockwise from the dealer.

•     Most casinos offer a side wager in this game. Betting that the original two cards will tie plays 10:1.
