Wild Aruba Stud Poker Game Rules

 

 

Brief Description

Wild Aruba Poker is a variation of five card stud poker in which players compete against the dealer. Wild Aruba Poker is played at a blackjack-size table seating no more than 7 players. Each player's position at the table has two betting areas marked Ante and Bet. Players can also choose to place a $1 side bet to participate in a progressive jackpot that substantially rewards rare poker hands.

 

Number of Players

Normally no more than 7 players participate.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

Standard 52-card deck, no jokers, Deuces (Twos) are wildcards.

 

Objective

To beat the dealer's five-card poker hand. Additionally, if you have placed the optional side bet for the progressive jackpot, to have a four of a kind or better poker hand.

How Suits Are Ranked

Suit rankings are not used in this game.

 

How Wild Aruba Stud Poker is Played

 

1.      Each player places a mandatory ante in the designated Ante area on the table in front of them. The ante must meet the table minimum

2.      If desired, the player can place an optional $1.00 side bet on the progressive jackpot.

3.      The dealer deals each player, including himself, five cards. The dealer's fifth card is dealt face up for all players to see.

4.      Each player evaluates their cards and declares whether they will fold (decide not to continue playing this hand) or bet (to remain in the game). Players who fold lose their ante, and this hand is over for them.

5.      Players who bet must place a bet in the designated Bet area on the table in front of them. This bet must be exactly double the amount of their ante.

6.      The dealer then reveals his best poker hand using all five of his cards. Two outcomes are then possible:

•     If the dealer's hand does not qualify with at least a pair of eights each remaining player receives even money on the ante, and the additional bet is returned.

•     If the dealer's hand does qualify with at least a pair of eights, then an individual comparison is made between each player's hand and the dealer's hand. If the dealer's hand is better than the player's hand, the player loses both the ante and additional bet. If the player's hand is better than the dealer's hand, the player wins even money on the ante, plus the additional bet according to the payoff table below. (Note that if the player's hand is identical to the dealer's hand, a tie is declared and no money is exchanged.)

Other Considerations and Options

•     Cards are dealt clockwise from the dealer.

•     Players should not look at their cards until all cards have been dealt.

•     Players cards must not be revealed to anyone else.

•     The dealer's hand qualifies by ranking a pair of eights or higher. In other words, the dealer's hand must contain a pair of eights, or it has to be strong enough to beat a poker hand that contains a pair of eights.

 

About Betting

 

•     The mandatory ante amount must meet the table minimum.

•     The bet placed in the designated Bet area on the table must be exactly double the amount of the ante. For example, if the ante was $5.00, the bet must be $10.00.

•     Winning ante bets are always paid even money, regardless of the player's hand
