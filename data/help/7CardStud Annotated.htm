Seven Card Stud Game Rules

 

Brief Description

Seven card stud is a poker game in which seven cards are dealt, although only five of those cards will be used to create the best possible poker hand. In Seven-Card Stud players receive a total of 3 down cards and 4 up cards.

 

Number of Players

To avoid running out of cards on a deal, no more than 7 players may participate.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

Standard 52-card deck, no jokers, no wildcards.

 

Objective

To have the highest ranking poker hand using five of your seven cards.

 

How Poker Hands are Ranked

From high to low:

 

•     Straight Flush (five cards in order, all of the same suit)

•     Four of a Kind

•     Full House (one three of a kind and one pair)

•     Flush (five cards of the same suit)

•     Straight (five cards in order, any suit)

•     Three of a Kind

•     Two Pair

•     One Pair

•     High Card

 

Note: Ace may be played high or low for a straight.

 

How Suits Are Ranked

Alphabetically from low to high:

•     Clubs

•     Diamonds

•     Hearts

•     Spades

 

How Seven Card Stud is Played

 

1.      Each player antes before the starting hand is dealt.

2.      Each player is dealt two cards face down (called hole cards or down cards), and one card face up (called the door card or up card).

3.      The person with the lowest door card opens this first round of betting (sometimes called the bring-in). When two or more cards are tied for low card, the card suits are used to break the tie. Betting continues clockwise from that person around the table.

4.      A second up card is dealt to all players remaining in the game. This fourth round is also known as Fourth Street.

5.      The highest up card starts this round of betting. The person with the best visible hand starts the bidding after the first hand In the event of a tie for the highest card, the person closest to the dealer's left goes first. Betting continues clockwise from that person around the table.

6.      Another up card is dealt to all players remaining in the game. This fifth round is also known as Fifth Street.

7.      The person with the highest up card starts this round of betting. In the event of a tie for the highest card, the person closest to the dealer's left goes first. Betting continues clockwise from that person around the table.

8.      Another up card is dealt to all players remaining in the game. This sixth round is also known as Sixth Street.

9.      The person with the highest up card starts this round of betting. In the event of a tie for the highest card, the person closest to the dealer's left goes first. Betting continues clockwise from that person around the table.

10. The last card is dealt face down to each of the remaining players. This seventh round is also known as Seventh Street.

11. The person with the highest up card again starts this last round of betting (sometimes called the river). In the event of a tie for the highest card, the person closest to the dealer's left goes first. Betting continues clockwise from that person around the table.

12. Time for showdown: all remaining players reveal their best poker hand. The winning poker hand takes the pot.

 

Other Considerations and Options

•     Cards are dealt clockwise from the dealer.

•     Players should look at their down cards as they are dealt. These cards must not be revealed to anyone else.

•     Players who fold their hand in the middle of a game must not reveal the contents of their folded hand. (Revealing a folded hand might benefit the remaining players, and would not be fair play.)

•     The player who made the first bet or last raise (if there were raises) shows their hand first at showdown. The show of hands continues clockwise around the table.

•     At showdown, you must show all seven of your cards, even though you will be using only five of them to create your best poker hand. You may be missing a better hand, and the other players have a right to see the cards you are not using.

•     If nobody calls or raises your final bet, you are not required to show any of your cards. (And no experienced player will ask to see them.)

 

About Betting

 

•     The person starting a betting round can choose to check  (not to bet), thereby passing the option to the next active player to his left. The player retains the option to see or raise once the betting comes back around. If every player around the table checks, the betting round is over and the next card is dealt. If any player bets, all players must respond to stay in the game.

•     After the first person bets, the player to his left must then see (match that bet), raise (increase the amount of the bet), or fold (drop out of the hand). This action then continues clockwise around the table to all active players.

•     A reraise is a raise of a previous raise. Betting ends when all who wish to see the highest bet have done so, or when all but one player has folded.
