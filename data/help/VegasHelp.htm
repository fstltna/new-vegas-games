Multiplayer Game

 

Hosting A Game

 

To host a game do the following:

1.  Enter the game room of the game you want to host. For example, go to the blackjack room, but do not click on a table.

 

 

 

 

2.  Click on the Internet Button on the control menu. Note that you should be connected to the internet or your network prior to clicking on the Internet Button

 

 

 

 

3.  Another dialog box will then appear asking if you want to Create, Join, or Cancel. To Create a game and have others join you, click on the Create Button.

 

 

4.  After you choose “Create” a table limits dialog box will appear. Select you table limits and click the OK button.

 

 

 

 

5.  After you have set your table limits, a lobby dialog box will appear. Wait here for others to join your game. When there are enough players, click on the Start button to begin the game.  After you click on the Start button, no other players will be able to join your table unless you repeat the steps to create another game.

 

 

 

 

Joining A Game

 

To join a game do the following:

1.  Enter the game room of the game you want to join. For example, go to the blackjack room, but do not click on a table yet.

 

 

 

 

2.  Click on the Internet Button on the control menu. Note that you should be connected to the internet or your network prior to clicking on the Internet Button

 

 

 

3.  Another dialog box will then appear asking if you want to Create, Join, or Cancel. To Join a game that is about to start, click on the Join Button.

 

 

4.  After you choose “Join” you will see a pink glow over tables where games are about to start. Click on one these tables to join a game.

 

 

 

5.  Once you have joined a game you will see a message saying “waiting for server to start game.” This means that you will be included in the game as soon as the creator of the game has gathered enough players into his/her lobby.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Trouble Shooting

 

I want people to join my game, but I can’t tell them what my IP address is.

 

 

To find you IP address:

1.  Go to the start menu and select Control Panel

2.  In the Control Panel, double click on the Network icon

3.  Look on the Identification Tab of the Network dialog box and find the field titled “Computer Name”

4.  This is your IP address

 

I want to join another player, but I think I have a bad connection

 

To Ping a computer:

1.  Go to the start menu and select “Run”

2.  At the Run screen, type: ping ip and the IP address.

3.  If there is no response (i.e. no numbers appear saying what the connection speed is), then you have a connection problem with the computer you are trying to play against.

4.  If you have a connection problem, be sure that you are properly connected to the internet or network that you are playing on. Also be sure to check that you have the proper IP address and that the computer you are looking for is actually online.

Seven Card Stud Game Rules

 

Brief Description

Seven card stud is a poker game in which seven cards are dealt, although only five of those cards will be used to create the best possible poker hand. In Seven-Card Stud players receive a total of 3 down cards and 4 up cards.

 

Number of Players

To avoid running out of cards on a deal, no more than 7 players may participate.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

Standard 52-card deck, no jokers, no wildcards.

 

Objective

To have the highest ranking poker hand using five of your seven cards.

 

How Poker Hands are Ranked

From high to low:

 

•     Straight Flush (five cards in order, all of the same suit)

•     Four of a Kind

•     Full House (one three of a kind and one pair)

•     Flush (five cards of the same suit)

•     Straight (five cards in order, any suit)

•     Three of a Kind

•     Two Pair

•     One Pair

•     High Card

 

Note: Ace may be played high or low for a straight.

 

How Suits Are Ranked

Alphabetically from low to high:

•     Clubs

•     Diamonds

•     Hearts

•     Spades

 

How Seven Card Stud is Played

 

1.       Each player antes before the starting hand is dealt.

2.       Each player is dealt two cards face down (called hole cards or down cards), and one card face up (called the door card or up card).

3.      The person with the lowest door card opens this first round of betting (sometimes called the bring-in). When two or more cards are tied for low card, the card suits are used to break the tie. Betting continues clockwise from that person around the table.

4.      A second up card is dealt to all players remaining in the game. This fourth round is also known as Fourth Street.

5.      The highest up card starts this round of betting. The person with the best visible hand starts the bidding after the first hand In the event of a tie for the highest card, the person closest to the dealer's left goes first. Betting continues clockwise from that person around the table.

6.      Another up card is dealt to all players remaining in the game. This fifth round is also known as Fifth Street.

7.      The person with the highest up card starts this round of betting. In the event of a tie for the highest card, the person closest to the dealer's left goes first. Betting continues clockwise from that person around the table.

8.      Another up card is dealt to all players remaining in the game. This sixth round is also known as Sixth Street.

9.      The person with the highest up card starts this round of betting. In the event of a tie for the highest card, the person closest to the dealer's left goes first. Betting continues clockwise from that person around the table.

10. The last card is dealt face down to each of the remaining players. This seventh round is also known as Seventh Street.

11. The person with the highest up card again starts this last round of betting (sometimes called the river). In the event of a tie for the highest card, the person closest to the dealer's left goes first. Betting continues clockwise from that person around the table.

12. Time for showdown: all remaining players reveal their best poker hand. The winning poker hand takes the pot.

 

Other Considerations and Options

•     Cards are dealt clockwise from the dealer.

•     Players should look at their down cards as they are dealt. These cards must not be revealed to anyone else.

•     Players who fold their hand in the middle of a game must not reveal the contents of their folded hand. (Revealing a folded hand might benefit the remaining players, and would not be fair play.)

•     The player who made the first bet or last raise (if there were raises) shows their hand first at showdown. The show of hands continues clockwise around the table.

•     At showdown, you must show all seven of your cards, even though you will be using only five of them to create your best poker hand. You may be missing a better hand, and the other players have a right to see the cards you are not using.

•     If nobody calls or raises your final bet, you are not required to show any of your cards. (And no experienced player will ask to see them.)

 

About Betting

 

•     The person starting a betting round can choose to check  (not to bet), thereby passing the option to the next active player to his left. The player retains the option to see or raise once the betting comes back around. If every player around the table checks, the betting round is over and the next card is dealt. If any player bets, all players must respond to stay in the game.

•     After the first person bets, the player to his left must then see (match that bet), raise (increase the amount of the bet), or fold (drop out of the hand). This action then continues clockwise around the table to all active players.

•     A reraise is a raise of a previous raise. Betting ends when all who wish to see the highest bet have done so, or when all but one player has folded.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Blackjack

 

Brief Description

Blackjack is a card game in which the objective is to have a hand value that is closer to twenty-one than that of the dealer, without going over twenty-one. All players play against the dealer. Blackjack is played at a semi-circle table with the dealer positioned on the flat side, and seven spots for players on the curved side. Each player's spot on the table is marked with a betting circle, and there are a few house rules printed in the middle of the table.

 

Other Names This Game Is Known As

Also called "Twenty-One."

 

Number of Players

Normally no more than 7 players participate.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

3 Standard 52-card decks, no jokers, no wildcards. Suits have no meaning in this game.

 

Objective

To be dealt cards having a count higher than those of the dealer, but not exceeding a total of twenty-one.

 

How Blackjack is Played

1.      Each player places his bet in the betting circle.

2.      The dealer, in turn, deals two cards to each player, including himself. The player's cards are both dealt face up. The dealer's first card is dealt face down, his second card is dealt face up for all players to see.

3.      In blackjack, the cards are valued as follows:  An Ace can count as either 1 or 11.  Any hand in which the Ace is currently counted as 11 is considered a soft hand.  The cards from 2 through 9 are valued as indicated. The 10, Jack, Queen, and King are all valued at 10.  The total value of the player’s hand is the sum of each card’s value.

4.      Each player evaluates his hand and, moving left to right from the dealer, makes one or more of the following decisions:

•     Surrender: Forfeit the hand with an automatic loss of half the original bet. (See "Other Considerations and Options.")

•     Hit (or Draw): To get an additional card. (Player's may choose this option more than once if necessary.)

•     Stand (or Hold): To decline any additional cards.

•     Double Down: The player can increase their bet up to double the initial bet in return for receiving one (and only one) additional card. (See "About Betting.").   The player can only double down on a pair of cards.

•     Split a Pair: In the case of two identical cards, a player can decide to split them into two separate hands. (See "Other Considerations and Options.")

•     Buy Insurance: When the dealer's upcard is an ace, players can make a side bet that the dealer's hole card will be a ten. (See "About Betting.")

•     Even Money:            If the player has a blackjack hand and the dealer's upcard is an ace, the dealer will offer to pay the player even money (1:1) before the dealer turns up his hole card. (See "About Betting.")

5.      The dealer reveals his hole card. If the total is 17 or more, the dealer must stand. If the total is 16 or less, the dealer must hit.

6.      Going from left to right the dealer compares his hand with those of the remaining players. Winners are paid immediately. Losing bets are collected immediately.

 

Other Considerations and Options

•     A blackjack, or natural is a total of 21 in your first two cards. (An ace plus a ten card.)

•     Cards are dealt clockwise from the dealer. The player to his left gets the first card.

•     Surrender: This option lets you surrender half your bet rather than continue your hand. If you are going to surrender you must do it right away. This option is not possible if you have taken any other action on your hand after the initial two cards are dealt. Also, surrender is only an option after the dealer has determined that his hand is not a blackjack.

•     Splitting a Pair: If your first two cards are identical, you can choose to play them as two independent hands. To do so, you must place an additional bet that is equal to your initial bet. The dealer will separate the cards and resolve each hand separately. If you split a pair and make 21 in two cards it is not considered a blackjack. (In other words it pays even money, not 3:2.)  When splitting Aces, most casinos will only allow a single card in addition to each ace.  Also, some casinos will allow a Double-Down bet on a split hand.

 

About Betting

 

•     A player's initial bet must meet the table minimum.

•     A winning blackjack (21 in the first two cards) pays 3:2 (1.5 times the bet). Other winning hands pay even money (1:1).

•     A player's blackjack beats any dealer total other than a dealer's blackjack, including a dealer's regular 21.

•     If both the player and the dealer have blackjack, the hand is a push.

•     Doubling Down: This option can only be taken if you have a two card hand. Doubling down lets you double the bet, and restricts you to taking only one more card. Any payouts or losses are based on the new total wager. The only exception to this would be if the dealer has blackjack, in which case you will only lose your original wager.

•     Insurance: If the dealer's upcard is an ace, you can buy insurance. Insurance is a side bet in which you are betting that the dealer's hole card will be a ten (adding up to blackjack). You can make a bet equal to one half of your initial bet. This bet pays 2:1.

•     Even Money: If your two upcards show blackjack, and the dealer's upcard is an ace, you can opt for an Even Money bet. This means the dealer will pay you even money (1:1) on your bet before he checks his  hole card for a possible blackjack. The rationale behind taking even money is that you could possibly tie with the dealer and win nothing at all.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Wild Aruba Stud Poker Game Rules

 

Brief Description

Wild Aruba Poker is a variation of five card stud poker in which players compete against the dealer. Wild Aruba Poker is played at a blackjack-size table seating no more than 7 players. Each player's position at the table has two betting areas marked Ante and Bet. Players can also choose to place a $1 side bet to participate in a progressive jackpot that substantially rewards rare poker hands.

 

Number of Players

Normally no more than 7 players participate.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

Standard 52-card deck, no jokers, Deuces (Twos) are wildcards.

 

Objective

To beat the dealer's five-card poker hand. Additionally, if you have placed the optional side bet for the progressive jackpot, to have a four of a kind or better poker hand.

2.      If desired, the player can place an optional $1.00 side bet on the progressive jackpot.

4.      Each player evaluates their cards and declares whether they will fold (decide not to continue playing this hand) or bet (to remain in the game). Players who fold lose their ante, and this hand is over for them.

5.      Players who bet must place a bet in the designated Bet area on the table in front of them. This bet must be exactly double the amount of their ante.

6.      The dealer then reveals his best poker hand using all five of his cards. Two outcomes are then possible:

If the dealer's hand is better than the player's hand, the player loses both the ante and additional bet. (Note that if the player's hand is identical to the dealer's hand, a tie is declared and no money is exchanged.)

•     Players should not look at their cards until all cards have been dealt.

Casino War

 

Brief Description

Casino War is a variation of the War game you might have played as a child. The player and the dealer each get one card, and the high card wins. If the cards are identical, you can choose to battle it out by going "to war".

 

Number of Players

This game is usually played on a standard blackjack table with one dealer and between one and seven players.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

This game is usually played with six standard 52-card decks, no jokers, and no wildcards.

 

Objective

To have the highest card and therefore win the "war".

 

How Suits Are Ranked

Suit rankings are not used in this game.

 

How Casino War is Played

1.      Each player places the opening bet before the first card is dealt.

2.      The cards are ranked as Ace being highest, 2 lowest.

3.      The dealer deals one card to each player and to himself, all face-up.

4.      One of the following will then happen:

·        If the dealer's card is higher than a player's card, that player loses his bet.

·        If a player's card is higher than the dealer's card, that player wins even money.

·        If the dealer's card matches a player's card, the player must either

·        Surrender (thereby losing half of his original bet, OR

·        Go to War (see instructions below).

 

Going To War

1.      To wage war, the player must double his original bet.

2.      The dealer will burn (put out of play, face down) three cards.

3.      The dealer will then deal one card to the player and one card to himself.

4.      One of the following will then happen:

·        If the dealer's second card is higher than the player's second card, that player loses both his original and additional bets.

·        If a player's second card is higher than (or matches) the dealer's second card, that player wins only his original bet.

 

Other Considerations and Options

•     Cards are dealt clockwise from the dealer.

•     Most casinos offer a side wager in this game. Betting that the original two cards will tie plays 10:1

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Let It Ride

 

Brief Description

Let It Ride is a variation of five card stud poker in which players don't play against each other or the dealer, but simply vie for one of eight winning poker hands. In Let It Ride, players receive 3 hole cards and use two community cards to create their poker hand. Let It Ride is played at a blackjack-size table seating no more than 7 players. Each player's position at the table has a payout schedule printed on it as well as three betting circles marked with a dollar sign ($), a number 2, and a number 1.

 

The name "Let It Ride" stems from the betting method used. All players begin the game by placing three equal bets on the table. Individually, at specified times in the game, two of those bets may be withdrawn or a player may choose to "let it ride" by leaving the bet on the table.

 

Number of Players

Normally no more than 7 players participate.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

Standard 52-card deck, no jokers, no wildcards. The deck is shuffled completely after each game.

 

Objective

To have the highest ranking five-card poker hand (containing at least a pair of tens or better), while maintaining the maximum bet on the table.

Suit rankings are not used in this game.

 

How Let It Ride is Played

 

1.      Each player antes before any cards are dealt. The ante must consist of three equal bets. Each bet is placed on one of the three betting circles marked on the table (marked $, 2, and 1). The table minimum is based on the base bet, not the total of all 3 bets.

2.      Each player is dealt three cards face down (called hole cards or down cards).

3.      The dealer deals two community cards face down in front of him. These cards will be used by all players, along with their three down cards to create a five-card poker hand.

4.      Players evaluate their three down cards and determine whether or not to keep the bet in the #1 betting circle. If they like their three cards, they can choose to let bet #1 ride. If they don't like their three cards, they can withdraw bet #1.

5.      The dealer reveals the first of the two community cards.

6.      Players evaluate their three cards in combination with the revealed community card and determine whether or not to keep the bet in the #2 betting circle. If they like their three cards in combination with the one community card showing, they can choose to let bet #2 ride. If they don't like their three cards in combination with the one community card showing, they can withdraw bet #2.

7.      The dealer reveals the second of the two community cards.

8.      Time for showdown: all players reveal their hands. Winning hands are those that match the payout schedule. Each remaining bet is paid based on this schedule.

Other Considerations and Options

•     Cards are dealt clockwise from the dealer.

About Betting

 

•     All bets must be of equal size. If you place a $10 bet in the $ betting circle, you must also place a $10 bet in the #2 betting circle and the #1 betting circle.

•     Your decision on whether or not to let bet #2 ride is completely independent of your decision on whether or not you let bet #1 ride. If you removed bet #1, you can still let bet #2 ride. If you removed bet #2, you can still let bet #1 ride.

•     You cannot remove the bet in the $ betting circle.

•     Being able to withdraw up to two of the three bets is logically equivalent to starting with one bet and being able to put out two more (by raising).

•     If your hand does not match any of those shown on the payout schedule, you lose all remaining bets on the table.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Omaha Hold ‘Em

 

Brief Description

Omaha Hold 'Em is a poker game in which nine cards are dealt, with five of those cards being used to create the best possible poker hand. In Omaha Hold 'Em players receive four down cards (which only they can see); while the other five cards are community cards, dealt face up in the middle of the table for everyone to see, and used by all players to create the best poker hand. In creating the best possible poker hand players are required to use two of their down cards in combination with three of the community cards.

 

Number of Players

From 3 to 11 players can participate.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

Standard 52-card deck, no jokers, no wildcards.

 

Objective

To have the highest ranking poker hand using two of the cards dealt to you in combination with three of the community cards.

 

How Poker Hands are Ranked

From high to low:

•     Straight Flush (five cards in order, all of the same suit)

•     Four of a Kind

•     Full House (one three of a kind and one pair)

•     Flush (five cards of the same suit)

•     Straight (five cards in order, any suit)

•     Three of a Kind

•     Two Pair

•     One Pair

•     High Card

 

Note: Ace may be played high or low for a straight.

 

How Suits Are Ranked

Alphabetically from low to high:

•     Clubs

•     Diamonds

•     Hearts

•     Spades

 

How Omaha Hold 'Em is Played

 

1.      A distinctive token, called the button, is put in front of one of the players. This button moves clockwise around the table with each new hand, and represents the "theoretical dealer." This is done so that no single player will always get the first card out, and to prevent any single player from always having to be the first one to make a bet. This is also done to make sure that all players at the table will eventually have to post the initial mandatory bets (described in the next step).

 

2.      Before any cards are dealt, the two people sitting to the left of the button must post two mandatory initial bets, called blinds. (They are called blinds because they take place before any cards are dealt, so players are blind to the possibilities of the hand.) The first player to the left of the button posts the small blind, which is usually equal to half of the minimum bet for the table. The next player to the left places the big blind, which is usually equal to the minimum bet for the table.

 

3.      Beginning with the player who posted the small blind, each player is dealt four cards face down (called hole cards or down cards).

 

4.      The player to the left of the person who posted the big blind now has one of three options: to see (match) the amount bet in the big blind; to raise (increase) the amount bet in the big blind; or to fold (drop out of the hand).

 

5.      The betting moves clockwise around the table until all players have exercised their options. Note that the players who posted the blinds only have to match the difference between the largest bet and their posted blind to remain in the game.

 

6.      The dealer lays out three community cards face up in the middle of the table. This is known as the flop.

 

7.      Another round of betting takes place, beginning with the first active player to the left of the button.

 

8.      The dealer lays out a fourth community card face up in the middle of the table. This card is also known as the turn.

 

9.      Another round of betting takes place, beginning with the first active player to the left of the button.

 

10. The dealer lays out a fifth community card face up in the middle of the table. This card is also known as the river.

 

11. Another round of betting takes place, beginning with the first active player to the left of the button.

 

12. Time for showdown: all remaining players reveal their best poker hand using three of the five community cards and two of the down cards they were dealt. The winning poker hand takes the pot. In the event of a tie, the pot is split (no other cards are used to break the tie).

 

Other Considerations and Options

•     Down cards are dealt clockwise, one at a time, beginning with the player who posted the small blind.

•     Players should look at their down cards as they are dealt. These cards must not be revealed to anyone else.

•     Players who fold their hand in the middle of a game must not reveal the contents of their folded hand. (Revealing a folded hand might benefit the remaining players, and would not be fair play.)

•     The player who made the last bet or raise shows their hand first at showdown. The show of hands continues clockwise around the table.

 

About Betting

 

•     The person starting a betting round can choose to check  (not to bet), thereby passing the option to the next active player to his left. The player retains the option to see or raise once the betting comes back around. If every player around the table checks, the betting round is over and the next card is dealt. If any player bets, all players must respond to stay in the game.

•     After the first person bets, the player to his left must then see (match that bet), raise (increase the amount of the bet), or fold (drop out of the hand). This action then continues clockwise around the table to all active players.

•     A reraise is a raise of a previous raise. Betting ends when all who wish to see the highest bet have done so, or when all but one player has folded.

•     Live Blinds are usually used in Omaha Hold 'Em. In other words, the blinds count as all or part of that players bet in the first round of betting. When the first round of betting reaches the player who placed the small blind, that player only has to add the amount necessary to see (match) the current bet, as he has already contributed to the pot for this round. If no raises have incurred during this round, the player who placed the big blind will owe nothing when the betting reaches him. He does have the option of raising the bet at this time. If he does not raise the bet, this round of betting is over. If he does raise the bet, each player in turn then once again has the option to see, raise, or fold.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Texas Hold ‘Em

 

Brief Description

Texas Hold 'Em is a poker game in which seven cards are dealt, with five of those cards being used to create the best possible poker hand. In Texas Hold 'Em players receive two down cards (which only they can see); while the other five cards are community cards, dealt face up in the middle of the table for everyone to see, and used by all players to create the best poker hand.

 

Other Names This Game Is Known As

Commonly called "Hold 'Em".

 

Number of Players

From 3 to 20 players can participate; most common in casinos are tables for 7-12 players.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

Standard 52-card deck, no jokers, no wildcards.

 

Objective

To have the highest ranking poker hand using five of the seven cards available to you.

 

How Poker Hands are Ranked

From high to low:

•     Straight Flush (five cards in order, all of the same suit)

7.      Another round of betting takes place, beginning with the first active player to the left of the button.

 

8.      The dealer lays out a fourth community card face up in the middle of the table. This card is also known as the turn.

 

9.      Another round of betting takes place, beginning with the first active player to the left of the button.

Pai Gow Poker

 

Brief Description

Pai Gow Poker is a game that combines American poker with an ancient Chinese dominoes game called Pai Gow (pronounced pie gow). Seven cards are dealt, and each player will form a five-card poker hand (called the high hand) and a two-card poker hand (called the low hand) from their seven cards. Pai Gow Poker is played at a blackjack-size table with betting spots for six players. Each player's position at the table includes a betting circle (usually marked with a number from 1 to 6), a horizontal rectangle to hold the player's two-card hand (usually labeled "2nd Highest"), and a vertical rectangle to hold the player's five-card hand (usually labeled "High" or "Highest").

 

Other Names This Game Is Known As

Sometimes called "Asian Poker" or "Double-Handed Poker."

 

Number of Players

No more than 7 players can participate (including the dealer).

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

Standard 52-card deck, plus one joker which acts as a limited wildcard. The joker can only be used as an ace, or to complete a straight, a flush, a royal flush or a straight flush.

 

Objective

For the player to have both their five-card and two-card poker hands rank higher than those of the dealer's. The player's five-card hand must beat the dealer's five-card hand. The player's two-card hand must beat the dealer's two-card hand.

 

How Poker Hands are Ranked

From high to low:

•     Royal Flush (ace-high straight flush)

•     Straight Flush (five cards in order, all of the same suit)

•     Four of a Kind

•     Full House (one three of a kind and one pair)

•     Flush (five cards of the same suit)

•     Straight (five cards in order, any suit)

•     Three of a Kind

•     Two Pair

•     One Pair

•     High Card

 

Note: Ace may be played high or low for a straight.

 

How Suits Are Ranked

Suit rankings are not used in this game.

 

Additional Pai Gow Ranking Rules

•     A player's low hand (the two-card hand) cannot be of a higher rank than their high hand (the five-card hand). If a player accidentally sets their hand so that the low hand ranks higher than the high hand, the hand is considered a foul and the player automatically loses that hand. For example, if the low hand consists of a pair of 8's, there must be a pair of 8's or better in the high hand. Remember: the objective here is to win with both the high hand and the low hand.

•     An ace-low straight (A-2-3-4-5) is the second highest straight (after A-K-Q-J-10). In other words, it would beat a king-high straight (K-Q-J-10-9).

 

How Pai Gow Poker is Played

 

1.      1.      Each player places a bet in the designated betting circle on the table in front of them.

2.      2.      The dealer deals seven cards to each place at the table, including his own, as well as any unoccupied places.

3.      3.      Each player arranges their hand into one five-card hand (the high hand), and one two-card hand (the low hand). Place the high hand face-down on the rectangle marked "High." Place the low hand face-down on the rectangle marked "2nd Highest."

4.      4.      The dealer turns over his cards and sets them according to the house rules. (House rules consist of guidelines dictating how the dealer creates his two hands. He must set his hand according to these guidelines.)

5.      5.      Each player's hands are turned over one at a time by the dealer and compared to the dealer's hand. The player's low hand is compared to the dealer's low hand, and the player's high hand is compared to the dealer's high hand. If the player and the dealer have exactly the same hand, it's called a copy. All copies are considered won by the dealer. There are three possible outcomes:

•     If both of the player's hands beat both of the dealer's hands, the bet will pay even money to the player, minus a 5% commission.

•     If both of the dealer's hands beat both of the player's hands, the player loses their bet. (Keep in mind that a copy is as good as a win for the dealer.)

•     If the player beats only one of the dealer's hands, this is considered a push (or tie), and no money changes hands. It doesn't matter whether the low hand or the high hand is the losing hand. Both hands are equal when determining the outcome of the wager.

 

Other Considerations and Options

•     On every deal 49 of the 53 total cards will be dealt. The leftover four cards go in the discard rack. Hands are dealt to all seven betting areas, including the dealer, whether they are occupied by a player or not. Players should not look at their cards until all cards have been dealt.

•     The dealer does not set their hand until all players have set theirs.

•     The high hand is evaluated just like any other five-card poker hand. (See "How Poker Hands Are Ranked" above, for more information.) The low hand is also ranked like other poker hands, but because this hand consists of only two cards, there can only be one of two rankings: a pair or high card. The highest possible low hand is a pair of aces.

•     The joker can only be used as an ace, or to complete a straight, a flush, a royal flush or a straight flush. If a joker is used to complete a flush or straight, the joker replaces the highest card missing from the flush or straight. For example, if a high hand consisted of the 2, 7, 9, and 10 of diamonds, the joker would act as the ace of diamonds to complete the flush. If a high hand consisted of the ace, jack, 5, and 2 of diamonds, the joker would act as the king of diamonds to complete the flush. If a high hand consisted of the 8 of clubs, 9 and 10 of hearts, and jack of spades, the joker would act as a queen (not the 7) to complete the straight. Additionally, the joker never forms pairs, three, four or five of a kind with any card other than an ace.

 

About Betting

 

•     Winning hands are paid even money less a 5% commission. For example, if a player bet $10 and they beat both of the dealer's hands, they would end up winning $9.50. The commission must be paid immediately.

•     No commission is charged on losing or push (tie) hands.

 

Red Dog

 

Brief Description

Red Dog is a game played with three community cards per game. All players win or lose at the same time. The first two cards are dealt face-up on the table. If the cards are nonconsecutive (and do not match) players then get the chance to bet on whether a third dealt card will fall within the range of the first two cards.

 

Other Names This Game Is Known As

Also known as Acey-Deucey, In-Between, Yablon, Ace-Deuce, In Between, and Between The Sheets

 

Number of Players

Between three and eight players at a time usually participate in this game.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

This game is usually played with six standard 52-card decks, no jokers, and no wildcards. The number of decks can vary.

 

Objective

To accurately guess whether the third card dealt will fall in the range between the first two cards dealt.

 

How Cards are Ranked in Red Dog

•     Cards from 2 to 10 count as face value

•     A Jack counts as 11

•     A Queen counts as 12

•     A King counts as 13

•     An Ace counts as 14

 

How Suits Are Ranked

Suit rankings are not used in this game.

How Red Dog is Played

 

1.      Each player places an opening bet before the first card is dealt. (This bet gets placed in the Bet circle on the table.)

 

2.      The dealer deals two community cards, face-up on the table.
These cards fall in one of three categories:

·        If the two cards match, then a third card is drawn. If the third card also matches the first two cards, players are paid off at 11:1. If the third card does not match the first two cards, the hand is considered a tie, you keep your money, and the hand is over.

·        If the two cards are consecutive, then the hand is considered a tie, you keep your money and the hand is over.

·        If the two cards are nonconsecutive, the dealer announces the spread between the two cards and gives players the option to raise their bet. This raise must be an amount equal to the original bet. In this instance, go to the step three.

3.     The dealer deals a third community card, face-up on the table.
One of two things can happen at this point:

·        If the third card is equal to either of the first two cards, or falls outside of the range, the players lose.

·        If the third card falls within the range , the players win. Typical payouts are listed below:

                                    Spread                                  Payout

1                                                                                            1                                                                                            5:1

2                                                                                            2                                                                                            4:1

3                                                                                            3                                                                                            2:1

                        4 through 11                           1:1

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Sic Bo

 

Brief Description

Sic Bo is an ancient Chinese dice game played with three six-sided dice. It is similar to Roulette in that there are many places on the game table to place bets and multiple bets can be placed. Each bet's payoff is printed on the table.

 

Objective

To accurately choose the individual numbers (or combination of numbers) that will appear on the dice after they are shaken.

 

How Sic Bo is Played

1.      Each player places their bet(s) on the table layout.
There are over fifty bets that can be made in seven categories. Each category is described below in the section titled "About Sic Bo Bets."

 

2.      The dealer throws the dice.

 

3.      The dealer clears the losing bets.

 

4.      The dealer pays the winning bets.

 

About Sic Bo Bets

There are seven different types of bets in Sic Bo:

 

Single Numbers

The bottom row of the Sic Bo table has six betting areas each showing a single die face numbered from 1 to 6. These are called single number bets, and are paid out as follows:

•  If one of the three dice comes up as one of the numbers you bet on, you win even money (1:1).

•  If two of the three dice come up as one of the numbers you bet on, you win double your money (2:1).

•  If three of the three dice come up as one of the numbers you bet on, you win triple your money (3:1).

Example:  If you bet $10.00 on the number 3, and two 3's came up in the dice roll, you would win $20.00.

 

Two-Number Combinations

Above the single number betting area is a row of Two-Number Combination bets (each showing two dice). Betting on these numbers means that you are betting that two of the three dice will match the combination you bet on. These bets pay 6:1.

Example:  If you bet $10.00 on the 3-4 combination, and the dice came up 3-4-5, you would win $60.00. If the dice came up 3-4-3, you would win twice ($120.00) because there are two combinations of 3-4.

 

Three-Number Totals

Above the two-number combination betting area is a row of Three-Number Total bets ranging from number 4 to number 17. Betting on these numbers means that you are betting that the three dice will equal the number you are betting on. The payoffs for these bets are shown in each betting square, and are noted below.

 

Three Number Total

Payoff

62:1

31:1

Example:  If you bet $10.00 on a total of 14, and the dice came up 5-5-4, you would win $120.00.

 

Small

At the top left corner of the Sic Bo table is the Small betting area. Betting Small means that you are betting that the dice roll will total between 4 and 10. This bet pays even money (1:1). Important note: a triplet (all three dice the same) will always result in a loss for the player regardless of the total.

Example:  If you bet $10.00 on Small, and the dice came up 3-1-2, you would win $10.00.

 

Big

At the top right corner of the Sic Bo table is the Big betting area. Betting Big means that you are betting that the dice roll will total between 11 and 17. This bet pays even money (1:1). Important note: a triplet (all three dice the same)  will always result in a loss for the player regardless of the total.

Example:  If you bet $10.00 on Big, and the dice came up 6-5-2, you would win $10.00.

 

Doubles

The top row of the Sic Bo table includes six betting areas for Doubles bets, ranging from double 1's to double 6's. Betting on any of the Doubles means that you are betting that two of the three dice will match the double you are playing. This bet pays 11:1.

Example:  If you bet $10.00 on double 3's, and the dice came up 3-3-2, you would win $80.00.

 

Triplets

The top row of the Sic Bo table includes seven betting areas for Triplet bets. Betting on a triple means that you are betting that all three dice come up the same. You can bet on a specific triple, which pays 180:1, or you can bet on Any Triple, which pays 31:1.

Example:  If you bet $10.00 on triple 3's, and the dice came up 3-3-3, you would win $180.00. If you bet on Any Triple, and the dice came up 7-7-7, you would win $310.00

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Royal Match 21

 

 

 

Brief Description

Royal Match 21 is a derivative of Blackjack. In this card game the objective is to have a hand value that is closer to twenty-one than that of the dealer, without going over twenty-one. All players play against the dealer. In addition to this, there is a side bet that can be made. If the first two cards dealt to a players are of the same suit, the player automatically wins. Royal Match 21 is played at a semi-circle table with the dealer positioned on the flat side, and seven spots for players on the curved side. Each player's spot on the table is marked with a betting circle, and there are a few house rules printed in the middle of the table.

 

Number of Players

Normally no more than 7 players participate.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

Six standard 52-card decks with all of the 10's removed (yielding six 48-card decks). Each deck consists of 2-9, jack, queen, king, and ace in all four suits, no jokers, no wildcards. Suits have no meaning in this game.

 

Objectives

To be dealt cards having a count higher than those of the dealer, but not exceeding a total of twenty-one.

 

To be dealt 2 cards of the same suit and have these cards dealt as your first two cards.

How Royal Match 21 is Played

Each player places his bet in the betting circle.

Each player places his side bet at the same time as the original bet.

The dealer, in turn, deals two cards to each player, including himself. The player's cards are both dealt face up. The dealer's first card is dealt face down, his second card is dealt face up for all players to see.

If a player is dealt 2 cards of the same suit on this initial deal, the side bet is automatically won.

The cards are valued as follows:  An Ace can count as either 1 or 11.  Any hand in which the Ace is currently counted as 11 is considered a soft hand.  The cards from 2 through 9 are valued as indicated. The Jack, Queen, and King are all valued at 10.  The total value of the player’s hand is the sum of each card’s value.

Each player evaluates his hand and, moving left to right from the dealer, makes one or more of the following decisions:

•     Surrender: Forfeit the hand with an automatic loss of half the original bet. (See "Other Considerations and Options.")

•     Hit (or Draw): To get an additional card. (Player's may choose this option more than once if necessary.)

•     Stand (or Hold): To decline any additional cards.

•     Double Down: Double the initial bet. (See "About Betting.")

•     Split a Pair: In the case of two identical cards, a player can decide to split them into two separate hands. (See "Other Considerations and Options.")

•     Buy Insurance: When the dealer's upcard is an ace, players can make a side bet that the dealer's hole card will be a ten. (See "About Betting.")

The dealer reveals his hole card. If the total is a hard 17 or more, the dealer must stand. If the total is a soft 17, or a total of 16 or less, the dealer must hit.

Going from left to right the dealer compares his hand with those of the remaining players. Winners are paid immediately. Losing bets are collected immediately.

 

Other Considerations and Options

•     A blackjack, or natural is a total of 21 in your first two cards. (An ace plus a ten card.)

•     Cards are dealt clockwise from the dealer. The player to his left gets the first card.

•     Surrender: This option lets you surrender half your bet rather than continue your hand. If you are going to surrender you must do it right away. This option is not possible if you have taken any other action on your hand after the initial two cards are dealt. Also, surrender is only an option after the dealer has determined that his hand is not a blackjack.

•     Splitting a Pair: If your first two cards are identical, you can choose to play them as two independent hands. To do so, you must place an additional bet that is equal to your initial bet. The dealer will separate the cards and resolve each hand separately. If you split a pair and make 21 in two cards it is not considered a blackjack. (In other words it pays even money, not 3:2.) You can split pairs up to four hands.

 

About Betting

 

•     A player's initial bet must meet the table minimum.

•     Player's blackjack always beats the dealer's blackjack, paying 3:2.

•     Player's total of 21 always beats the dealer's total of 21. In other words, a player's 4-10-7 beats the dealer's 5-9-7.

•     A winning blackjack (21 in the first two cards) pays 3:2 (1.5 times the bet). Other winning hands pay even money (1:1), with the exception of those meriting bonus payoffs (described below).

•     Doubling Down: This option can be taken regardless of the number of cards in your hand. Doubling down lets you double the bet, and in Royal Match 21 you are not restricted to taking only one more card. Any payouts or losses are based on the new total wager. The only exception to this would be if the dealer has blackjack, in which case you will only lose your original wager.

•     Players can opt for a Double Down Rescue. If you are dissatisfied with the doubled hand, you can take back the doubled portion of the bet and forfeit the original bet. (This does not apply to busted hands.)

•     Insurance: If the dealer's upcard is an ace, you can buy insurance. Insurance is a side bet in which you are betting that the dealer's hole card will be a ten (adding up to blackjack). You can make a bet equal to one half of your initial bet. This bet pays 2:1.

•     Royal Match Bet: If the first two cards dealt to a players are of the same suit, the player automatically wins.
