Let It Ride Game Rules

 

Brief Description

Let It Ride is a variation of five card stud poker in which players don't play against each other or the dealer, but simply vie for one of eight winning poker hands. In Let It Ride, players receive 3 hole cards and use two community cards to create their poker hand. Let It Ride is played at a blackjack-size table seating no more than 7 players. Each player's position at the table has a payout schedule printed on it as well as three betting circles marked with a dollar sign ($), a number 2, and a number 1.

 

The name "Let It Ride" stems from the betting method used. All players begin the game by placing three equal bets on the table. Individually, at specified times in the game, two of those bets may be withdrawn or a player may choose to "let it ride" by leaving the bet on the table.

 

Number of Players

Normally no more than 7 players participate.

 

Description of Deck(s) Used

Standard 52-card deck, no jokers, no wildcards. The deck is shuffled completely after each game.

 

Objective

To have the highest ranking five-card poker hand (containing at least a pair of tens or better), while maintaining the maximum bet on the table.

How Suits Are Ranked

Suit rankings are not used in this game.

 

How Let It Ride is Played

 

1.      Each player antes before any cards are dealt. The ante must consist of three equal bets. Each bet is placed on one of the three betting circles marked on the table (marked $, 2, and 1). The table minimum is based on the base bet, not the total of all 3 bets.

2.      Each player is dealt three cards face down (called hole cards or down cards).

3.      The dealer deals two community cards face down in front of him. These cards will be used by all players, along with their three down cards to create a five-card poker hand.

4.      Players evaluate their three down cards and determine whether or not to keep the bet in the #1 betting circle. If they like their three cards, they can choose to let bet #1 ride. If they don't like their three cards, they can withdraw bet #1.

5.      The dealer reveals the first of the two community cards.

6.      Players evaluate their three cards in combination with the revealed community card and determine whether or not to keep the bet in the #2 betting circle. If they like their three cards in combination with the one community card showing, they can choose to let bet #2 ride. If they don't like their three cards in combination with the one community card showing, they can withdraw bet #2.

7.      The dealer reveals the second of the two community cards.

8.      Time for showdown: all players reveal their hands. Winning hands are those that match the payout schedule. Each remaining bet is paid based on this schedule.

Other Considerations and Options

•     Cards are dealt clockwise from the dealer.

•     Players should look at their down cards as they are dealt. These cards must not be revealed to anyone else.

 

About Betting

 

•     All bets must be of equal size. If you place a $10 bet in the $ betting circle, you must also place a $10 bet in the #2 betting circle and the #1 betting circle.

•     Your decision on whether or not to let bet #2 ride is completely independent of your decision on whether or not you let bet #1 ride. If you removed bet #1, you can still let bet #2 ride. If you removed bet #2, you can still let bet #1 ride.

•     You cannot remove the bet in the $ betting circle.

•     Being able to withdraw up to two of the three bets is logically equivalent to starting with one bet and being able to put out two more (by raising).

•     If your hand does not match any of those shown on the payout schedule, you lose all remaining bets on the table.
